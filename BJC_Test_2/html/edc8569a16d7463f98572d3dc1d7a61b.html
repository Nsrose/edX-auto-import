CS10: Impact Post

Your goal for this assignment is to explore the broad field of computing and its implications on society by writing a Impact post aimed at teaching others about a computing-related topic that you're interested in. You will have a large degree of freedom about the topic of your article, and you will be posting it publicly for the rest of the class to comment on.

Requirements

  • Impact Post
  • Written Post
  • Artifact and 3 Comments 

Written Submission

Picking A Topic

Think of an innovation in computing that has changed the world. You have four broad prompts to choose from:

  1. Old topic, new angle. You can choose to delve into a topic that we've already discussed in class. You may focus on one aspect of this topic to explore in greater depth.
  2. New topic. If you choose to write about a topic that we have not talked about in class, research the topic and develop a good understanding of how it works. You can write about the particular implications of a technology without delving too far into the technology itself if you prefer.
  3. CS + X. Choose another field of study, hobby, or profession and describe how computing has affected it in the past couple of decades. You could choose, for example, the effects of computing in economics and discuss high-frequency trading.
  4. Debates in technology. Discuss a debate related to technology. This could be either a current or historical debate. Although you should choose a side (and explain why you chose this side), you should discuss the merits and demerits of both sides of the argument equally so that readers have a clear view of both sides.

Feel free to run your topic by your TA if you're concerned about its relevance. Chances are high that it's fine if it's related to technology and one of the categories mentioned above.

Written Submission Requirements

  • Describe how the innovation has caused a social, economic, or cultural impact on a population and discuss its significance
  • Describe the beneficial as well as any harmful effects of the innovation you explored. For example, you could discuss the security concerns, or the social implications of your innovation.
  • Describe at a high level the technology behind your chosen innovation. This can include levels of abstraction, relevant algorithms, and any relationships to other innovations (i.e. the Internet).

Artifact

What’s an artifact you say? As cool as it would be for you to present a lost treasure like a modern Indiana Jones, you probably don’t have to time for an excavation.

Instead your artifact should be a creative accompaniment to your written submission. This could be done in many forms. Here are some examples:

  • A Snap! program that demonstrates a simulation or application of your innovation.
  • A sleek infographic or presentation (Powerpoint, Prezi, etc.) that displays relevant data about your innovation.
  • A song, video, skit, poem, or other type of live presentation that highlights certain aspects of your innovation
  • A 3D-printed object related to your innovation (this may be difficult to do though).
  • Something else entirely!
  • Click here for an example the TAs made on how to make an artifact!

Be creative, and ask your TA if you have an unconventional idea that you’d like to try, but are unsure if it is sufficient.

Rules & Guidelines

There are a few rules and guidelines that you have to stick to after you've chosen your topic.

  • Rule #1: Your post must be at least four healthy paragraphs long. Feel free to go beyond that, but we seriously doubt that any topic that is covered in less space will be adequately described. There’s a minimum of 550 words. There's also a soft limit around 700 words...try not to exceed this by too much. ;-)
  • Rule #2: You must cite at least three (3) different sources. Feel free to use printed texts, readings from class, or textbooks from other classes. You can also use news articles from reputable sources or Wikipedia if it's appropriate for your topic, but at least one source must be from somewhere other than Wikipedia. A full MLA/APA-style citation is not necessary here; a simple footnote with the URL and author/publication is sufficient.
  • Rule #3: You have to write the Impact post. Do not copy and paste someone else's work. Plagiarism is a serious issue and it is best for everyone if we don't have to cross that bridge.
  • Guideline #1: Feel free to use diagrams, tables, embedded videos, or any other media if you think that it helps to communicate your point. You are not required to use anything other than text.
  • Guideline #2: Spend some time thinking about what you're going to say before you say it. Since the length requirement for the post is relatively small, we're looking for quality over quantity in your work. Quality implies that you have well-researched and insightful observations and speak meaningfully about the topic you've chosen to discuss.

Comments

You will be required to comment on at least three posts written by other students. The comments should be done as follows:

  • Comment on the post below you
  • Comment on the post above you
  • Comment on a post of your choice

You are not expected to perform any additional research for your comments, but should provide some sort of insightful feedback that shows that you have read and (hopefully) understood the author's post (i.e. “Nice post” is not a sufficient comment). Each comment should be at least one paragraph long. Add properly formatted links to your Impact post pointing at the posts with your comments.
