Overview

Big Ideas

  • There are more to HOFs than just map, keep, and combine.
  • Functions can be treated as data, just like strings, numbers, and lists.
  • Creating our own higher-order function blocks allows us to achieve a whole new level of generality in our programs.

Activites

  • Build non-HOF blocks to use as helper functions in larger HOFs problems.
  • Complete complex, multi-step HOFs problems.
  • Use HOFs to solve real-life problems, like minimizing or composing functions.
